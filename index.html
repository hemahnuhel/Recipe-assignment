Chicken Biryani

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish layered with aromatic basmati rice, spiced chicken, and fresh herbs. This royal dish is a perfect choice for festive occasions, family gatherings, or any time you crave a special homemade meal.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 30 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken stock or water
  • Saffron strands soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the onions until golden brown. Remove half and set aside for garnishing.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste to the remaining onions in the pot and sauté for 1 minute. Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Mix in chopped tomatoes, yogurt, biryani masala, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Cook until chicken is tender and the oil begins to separate.
  5. In a separate pot, boil water or chicken stock and cook the soaked rice until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  6. Layer half of the rice over the chicken mixture, sprinkle with mint, coriander, and half of the saffron milk.
  7. Top with the remaining rice, saffron milk, and fried onions. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 20–25 minutes.
  8. Gently mix before serving hot with raita or salad.

Tips

For authentic flavor, use aged basmati rice and cook on a very low flame during the final steaming stage to let the aromas fully develop.
